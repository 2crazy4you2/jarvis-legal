Jarvis — Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 10, 2026

What Data We Collect

When you connect your social media accounts to Jarvis, we collect the following information:

  • Account information — Your display name and profile identifier, used to show which account content will be published to.
  • Authentication tokens — OAuth access and refresh tokens required to publish content on your behalf.
  • Content you upload — Video files, captions, thumbnails, and publishing settings that you provide when creating a post.
  • Publishing metadata — Records of posts made through Jarvis, including timestamps, platform, and publish status.

How We Use Your Data

Your data is used exclusively to provide the Jarvis publishing service:

  • To authenticate with social media platforms on your behalf.
  • To upload and publish video content to the platforms you select.
  • To schedule content for future publishing at times you specify.
  • To display the status of your posts (processing, published, failed).

We do not use your data for advertising, profiling, or any purpose other than providing the service.

Data Storage and Security

All user data, including authentication tokens and publishing history, is stored locally on the device running Jarvis. Video files are cached temporarily during the upload process and are deleted after publishing is complete.

No user data is stored on external servers. Authentication tokens are stored securely on the local device and are never transmitted to any party other than the respective social media platform's API.

Data Sharing

We do not sell, share, or distribute your data to any third party. Your content is transmitted only to the social media platforms you explicitly choose to publish to (e.g., TikTok, YouTube, Instagram, Facebook) and only when you initiate the publish action.

Third-Party Services

Jarvis integrates with the following third-party services:

  • TikTok — Login Kit and Content Posting API, used for authentication and video publishing.
  • YouTube — Data API v3, used for video uploads.
  • Facebook — Graph API, used for video publishing.
  • Instagram — Graph API, used for video publishing.

Use of these services is governed by their respective terms of service and privacy policies.

User Rights

You have the right to:

  • Access — View all data Jarvis has stored related to your accounts.
  • Delete — Remove your connected accounts and all associated data at any time.
  • Revoke — Disconnect platform integrations, which immediately stops Jarvis from accessing those accounts.

Content Posting

Jarvis only publishes content when explicitly initiated by the user. All post metadata — including captions, privacy levels, interaction settings, and commercial content disclosure — is fully controlled by the user before publishing. Jarvis does not modify, watermark, or add branding to user content.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Continued use of Jarvis after changes are posted constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
